1. Getting Started

CareerForge runs as four separate web portals, one per persona. Each portal is gated by a dedicated Keycloak realm, so you log in to the portal that matches your role.

PortalURLRealmAudience
Employeecf-employee.localemployeeAll employees, plus managers
Coachcf-coach.localcoachL&D coaches and mentors
Admincf-admin.localadminHR admins, platform admins
Slidescf-slides.localnoneAnyone (this site)

All demo passwords are Test1234!

The full list of usernames is on the Demo Accounts page. Pick a username matching the persona you want to explore.

Login flow

  1. Open the portal URL in your browser.
  2. You will be redirected to Keycloak (http://keycloak.local) with a PKCE OIDC flow.
  3. Enter your username and Test1234!. Click Sign In.
  4. Keycloak redirects you back to the portal with an access token, and you land on the dashboard.

2. The Employee Portal

The Employee portal is your personal workspace. Eight navigation items by default, plus a ninth (My Team) that only appears if your account has the manager role.

1

Dashboard

Industry 4.0 readiness score, top skill gaps, learning path progress, recent commitments.

2

Learning Path

Node-by-node visualisation of your development journey. Click a node to trigger AI-generated content.

3

Opportunities

Browse internal openings: bench, L&D rotations, R&D experiments. Apply with one click.

4

My Applications

Track every opportunity you have applied to, with status, watchers, and message thread.

5

Dev Plan

Timeline view of your IDP. Goals, milestones, target skills, sign-off status.

6

Commitments

Personal development commitments. Mark in progress / completed. Activity score and inactivity alarm.

7

My Profile

Edit your skills, certifications, learning preferences, and contact info. PII fields are encrypted.

8

Notifications

Coach PII consent requests, IDP updates, opportunity matches, manager sign-off requests.

4. The Coach Portal

Coaches see employees through an anonymised lens. Real names and contact details are never shown unless an employee explicitly grants a consent token.

Coach navigation

  1. Catalogue — List of all employees (anonymised). Filter by OU, seniority, specialisation, readiness band. Skill heatmap visualisation.
  2. Employee Detail — Click any employee to open an anonymised profile: pseudonymous ID, role, skills, learning path, commitments timeline. Request PII reveal here.
  3. Forms — Author IDPs, 1:1 agendas, feedback forms, nominations. Add commitments inline. Track status.
  4. Opportunities — Create and manage internal openings. Review applications.
  5. Analytics — Three tabs: overview, Industry 4.0 readiness (per-OU), market trends.
  6. Audit Log — Click any entry to expand. Export to CSV or JSON.

Requesting PII reveal

From any anonymised employee profile, click Request PII Reveal. The employee receives a notification and must accept or decline. Once accepted, the coach gets a short-lived consent token that decrypts the PII for that single session. Every request and reveal is logged.

5. The Admin Portal

Admins manage the platform itself: taxonomy, users, AI connectors, and global analytics.

  1. Skill Taxonomy — Manage the canonical skill list. Categories, levels, descriptions. The taxonomy drives the readiness scoring.
  2. Employee Management — CRUD employees, bulk import via CSV.
  3. Connectors — Configure AI data sources from a visual catalog (11 templates). Trigger sync, view ingestion stats per connector.
  4. Analytics — Platform overview: active employees, coaches, connectors, AI documents, 24h ingestion chunks.
  5. Readiness — Company-wide score gauge with industry benchmark comparison.
  6. Audit Log — Same audit log as the coach portal, with admin-level filters.

6. The IDP Lifecycle

An Individual Development Plan needs three signatures. CareerForge models this as an explicit state machine.

draft  →  awaiting_employee  →  awaiting_coach  →  awaiting_manager  →  signed
  1. Coach drafts the IDP in the Coach portal, attaching goals, target skills, and a timeline.
  2. Employee commits to the IDP in the Employee portal — reviews goals and accepts.
  3. Coach signs off — validates that the plan aligns with the employee's career trajectory.
  4. Manager signs off — from the My Team tab, approves or sends back with feedback.
  5. Once all three signatures are in place, the IDP becomes signed and commitments start to show on the employee's timeline.

7. Working with the AI Assistant

CareerForge's AI features are co-produced — every suggestion is grounded in real connector documents (Stack Overflow questions, GitHub trending repos, Medium articles, etc.). Hallucination is minimised by RAG.

Where AI shows up

  • Employee dashboard — AI-suggested next roles based on current skills + market trends
  • Learning path — AI-generated learning materials per skill node
  • Coach forms — AI-suggested goals and target skills based on the employee's profile
  • Admin connectors — Sync triggers, RAG document ingestion, intelligence digest

Refreshing AI content

AI responses are cached with TTLs from 6 hours (market trends) to 7 days (intelligence digest). To force a fresh response, the admin can trigger a connector sync from the Connectors page.

8. Troubleshooting

I cannot log in

  • Make sure you are using the correct portal for your persona. alice.johnson only works at cf-employee.local, not cf-coach.local.
  • Check that cf-employee.local, cf-coach.local, cf-admin.local, and keycloak.local are in your hosts file (C:\Windows\System32\drivers\etc\hosts on Windows).
  • Clear your browser cookies for keycloak.local and try again.

Manager tab is missing

  • Only five users have the manager role: ethan.moore, fiona.taylor, edward.hughes, xena.phillips, yusuf.campbell.
  • Log out and back in to refresh the JWT — role changes only apply to new tokens.

AI suggestions are empty

  • Open the Admin portal → Connectors and click Sync on any active connector. After ingestion completes, the AI will have grounding documents to draw from.
  • If sync reports "0 chunks ingested", that is correct — the dedup filter detected that all documents were already in the vector store.

Page shows old data after a fresh deployment

  • Your browser may have cached old assets. Hard refresh with Ctrl+Shift+R.
  • If the new build was just imported, the deployment may still be rolling. Check kubectl get pods -n careerforge.
