06 — Safety & Compliance

10 AI Guardrails

Production-grade safety controls for responsible AI deployment

  1. 01 Confidence threshold — 60% minimum; auto-reject low-confidence suggestions
  2. 02 Rate limiting — max 5 analysis calls/hour (Redis-backed, HTTP 429)
  3. 03 Content filter — PII redaction (email, phone, SSN, card, IBAN)
  4. 04 Prompt injection detection — screening on all AI output before processing
  5. 05 Product catalog cap — max 50 active products to prevent noise
  6. 06 Dual approval — different admin must implement vs. approve
  7. 07 24-hour staging — cool-down period before AI products go live
  8. 08 Auto-expiry — stale suggestions expire after 30 days
  9. 09 Fairness monitoring — Prometheus histograms by age bracket and income tier
  10. 10 Audit immutability — PostgreSQL triggers block UPDATE/DELETE on audit tables; public transparency endpoint (AI Act Art. 13); kill switch with full audit trail
07 — Auditability

Comprehensive Audit System

Four immutable audit tables with database-level protection

Table Purpose Regulation
audit_log Central, immutable, append-only log of every state-changing action. Records actor, action, resource, before/after changes, IP, user-agent, endpoint, HTTP method/status, duration, and correlation request_id. GDPR Art. 30
ai_api_call_log Every AI provider interaction: request prompt, response text, model, provider, tokens used, latency, HTTP status, guardrail outcome, and errors. AI Act Art. 12
consent_history Append-only log of every consent flag change with old/new values, who changed it, and when — proof of consent. GDPR Art. 7(1)
audit_recommendations Full snapshot of every offer generation: input features, profile result, suitability checks, scored products, exclusions, consent state, model version. AI Act Art. 12

Immutability enforced at the database level: PostgreSQL BEFORE UPDATE OR DELETE triggers on all 4 audit tables. The offer engine returns HTTP 500 if the audit write fails — recommendations cannot be served without an audit record (AI Act Art. 12).

Three steps from registration to personalized offers

GDPR Art. 6 · GDPR Art. 22 · AI Act Art. 13

→
2

Profile Questionnaire

Age, annual income, dependents, employment status, risk tolerance, homeowner status, and existing banking products. Used by the profiler to classify life stage and risk score.

Life Stage · Risk Score · Segments

→
3

Review & Confirm

Full summary of consent selections and profile data. On confirmation, the system generates a computed profile and the customer immediately receives personalized offers.

Auto Profile · Instant Offers

Privacy by design: New customers receive auto-increment IDs (51, 52, ...). Email is never stored — only an irreversible SHA-256 hash. Internal IDs are replaced with pseudonymized UUIDs for external use. Data is automatically anonymized after 2 years.
