Section 01

What BankOffer AI Does

The platform ingests real-time transaction streams, builds a per-customer feature profile, scores every active product against that profile, enforces regulatory compliance rules, and delivers a ranked, personalised offer through push notification, email, or in-app banner — all within a single pipeline run triggered by a customer event.

01

Event Trigger

Transaction, login, or lifecycle event received from core banking

Kafka · CDC
02

Customer Profile

20 real-time features: balance trend, spending, risk tolerance, age segment

Feast · Redis · PostgreSQL
03

Product Scoring

XGBoost model scores each active product against the customer profile

scikit-learn · MLflow
04

Compliance Filter

Suitability rules: MiFID II, IDD, GDPR, AI Act hard filters applied per offer

Rules engine · Audit log
05

Offer Ranking

Top N offers ranked by composite score, personalised copy generated

FastAPI · Ranking model
06

Delivery

Push notification, email, or in-app banner sent to the customer

FCM · SES · WebSocket
07

Customer accepts, views details, or declines. Suitability confirmation collected where required

Portal · MiFID consent
08

Feedback Loop

Outcome recorded, model retrains weekly on new acceptance/rejection signal

Airflow · MLflow registry
Regulatory Coverage

Built-in Compliance

Every recommendation is filtered through hard-coded suitability rules before delivery. Products ineligible for a customer are excluded and logged with a reason code, creating a permanent audit trail compliant with EBA and AI Act Article 12–13.

MiFID II IDD GDPR Art. 22 AI Act High-Risk EBA Guidelines

Pluggable AI Layer

Section 02

Admin Portal

The Admin Portal (/admin) is the central control plane for bank staff. It provides 12 sections covering system health, user management, regulatory compliance, product configuration, AI connector management, market intelligence, and financial performance analytics.

Admin › Overview

Dashboard

Live system health: API latency, active products, pending AI suggestions, compliance statistics, connector status, and recent audit events.

How to use
  1. Land here after login to verify system status
  2. Check "Pending AI Suggestions" count — any items waiting for approval
  3. Review compliance pass rate and flag count for quick health check
Admin › Users

User Management

View and manage bank staff and customer accounts sourced from Keycloak. Inspect roles, last login, MFA status, and linked customer profile.

How to use
  1. Search by name or email using the filter bar
  2. Click a row to inspect roles and linked customer ID
  3. Verify MFA is enabled for admin-role accounts
Admin › Security

Security & MFA

Configure authentication policy, social login providers (Google, Microsoft, Facebook), MFA enforcement, session timeouts, and brute-force protection settings.

How to use
  1. Enable or disable social login providers per bank policy
  2. Set MFA as required for admin roles
  3. Adjust session idle/max lifetime under "Session Policy"
Admin › AI Act

AI Act Compliance

Risk register for the high-risk AI system classification, compliance deadline tracking, suitability rule documentation, and consent statistics. Required for EU AI Act Article 9–15 conformity assessment.

How to use
  1. Review the Risk Register entries before any model update
  2. Check consent statistics to ensure opt-out rates are within thresholds
  3. Export the technical documentation section before an audit
Admin › Tokens

API Tokens

Create and revoke API tokens for system-to-system integrations (core banking, CRM, external analytics). Token scopes can be restricted to specific endpoints.

How to use
  1. Click "Generate Token" and select the required scope
  2. Copy the token immediately — it is not shown again
  3. Revoke any token that is no longer needed or has been exposed
Admin › Products

Product Catalog

Manage the full catalogue of banking products available for recommendation. Add, edit, activate or deactivate products. Each product has type, risk level, category, priority, and suitability metadata.

How to use
  1. Click "Add Product" and fill in all required fields including risk level
  2. Toggle active/inactive to include or exclude from the scoring engine
  3. Inactive products are never recommended but remain in the audit history

Admin Portal — Intelligence, Performance & Audit

The second half of the admin portal covers the AI-driven capabilities: market intelligence, AI connector configuration, product performance analytics, and the full recommendation audit trail with per-record drill-down.

Admin › Consent

Consent Registry

Manage GDPR consent rules per product and channel. View the product-consent matrix showing which consent is required for each product. Track consent sync status with the customer-facing portal.

How to use
  1. Open the Product Consent Matrix to see consent requirements at a glance
  2. Add a new consent rule by selecting a product and the required consent type
  3. Verify "Last Synced" timestamp — should be within 24 hours
Admin › Connectors

AI Connectors

Configure external AI and data provider integrations. Each connector has a status lifecycle: Available → Pending Approval → Approved → Active → Disabled. Active connectors are used by the Intelligence Hub and Playbook Analysis.

How to use
  1. Click a connector card to open the configuration panel
  2. Enter the API key and select the model version
  3. Submit for approval — a compliance officer reviews before activation
  4. Only connectors in "Active" state are called by the AI features
Admin › Intelligence

Intelligence Hub

AI-generated market intelligence (exchange rates, regulations, economic indicators, geopolitics) and product suggestions. Suggestions go through a human-in-the-loop approval workflow before they can be added to the product catalogue.

How to use
  1. Click "Analyse Market" to trigger an AI call (rate-limited: 5/hour)
  2. Review generated product suggestions in the pending queue
  3. Approve → product is added to the catalog; Reject → logged with reason
  4. Filter by category (exchange markets, regulations, trends…)
Admin › Audit Log

Audit Trail

Immutable log of every recommendation batch generated by the engine. Each record shows the customer UUID, trigger signal, timestamp, number of offers generated/excluded, and suitability check results. Required by EBA and AI Act Art. 12.

How to use
  1. Filter by date range or customer UUID to locate a specific decision
  2. Click a Rec ID to open the detail panel: suitability checks (PASS/FAIL per rule), final offer cards, and excluded products with reason codes
  3. Use the raw JSON export for regulatory audit submissions
Admin › Product P&L

Product P&L Dashboard

Per-product performance scorecard over a selectable period (7d / 30d / 90d). Shows offers generated, funnel conversion, compliance pass rate, composite score (Business Score − Risk Penalty), and net contribution. Includes an AI-powered playbook analysis on demand.

How to use
  1. Select the period (7d / 30d / 90d) — all metrics recalculate
  2. Use filter buttons above the table: All, Low Conversion, High Block Rate, Negative Score, High Complaints
  3. Read the Position Map: X = Final Score, Y = Compliance %, bubble = volume
  4. Click "Run AI Analysis" in the Playbook section to get per-product AI recommendations from the active connector
Admin › Config

System Configuration

Runtime configuration panel for the scoring engine: confidence thresholds, rate limits, compliance rule toggles, model kill-switch, product catalogue cap, and cooldown periods for AI-suggested products.

How to use
  1. Adjust confidence threshold (default 0.60) to tighten or loosen offer eligibility
  2. Use the model kill-switch to immediately halt all AI recommendations in an incident
  3. Set cooldown hours for new AI-suggested products before they go live

Section 03

Customer Portal

The Customer Portal (/portal) is the customer-facing interface. Customers log in with their bank credentials (Keycloak SSO) and see their personalised offers, the data used to generate them, their GDPR consent settings, interaction history, and any active application forms.

Portal › Offers

My Offers

Displays the customer's current personalised product recommendations. Each offer card shows the product name, a personalised explanation, and a call-to-action. Products excluded by the compliance filter are shown in a collapsed "Suitability Filter" section with a GDPR Art. 22 transparency notice explaining why they were excluded.

Customer journey
  1. Customer sees offer cards ranked by relevance score
  2. Tapping "Learn More" starts the product journey and records a journey_started event
  3. For investment products, a MiFID II suitability confirmation modal appears before acceptance
  4. Accepting an offer creates an application form and routes to the Forms section
  5. Declining records the event in the feedback loop for model improvement
Portal › My Data

My Data

Full transparency view of the customer features used by the recommendation engine. Shows each feature name, value, and a plain-language explanation of how it influences recommendations. Required by GDPR Art. 22 (right to explanation for automated decisions).

Customer journey
  1. Customer can see their current risk profile, balance trend, spending segments
  2. Each feature has a tooltip explaining its source (transaction history, profile)
  3. No raw data (account numbers, amounts) is shown — only derived features
  4. Customer can request a data correction via the support flow from this page
Portal › Consent

Consent Management

GDPR consent hub. Customers can review, grant, or withdraw consent for each processing purpose: personalised recommendations, data analysis, marketing communications, profiling, and MiFID II suitability processing. Each consent change is timestamped and synced to the admin Consent Registry.

Customer journey
  1. Toggle individual consent purposes on or off
  2. Withdrawing "Personalised Recommendations" stops new offers immediately
  3. Each toggle generates a consent event logged in the audit trail
  4. MiFID II suitability consent is presented per-offer, not as a blanket toggle
Portal › History

Recommendation History

Chronological log of all offers presented to the customer. Shows product name, date, action taken (viewed / accepted / declined), and outcome. Customers have a right to access this history under GDPR Art. 15.

Customer journey
  1. Browse the full history of offers received
  2. Filter by product type or date range
  3. Accepted offers show the current application status
  4. Declined offers can be re-evaluated if the customer changes their mind via the offer cards section
Portal › Forms

Application Forms

Digital product application forms generated when a customer accepts an offer. Form fields are pre-populated from the customer profile where allowed. The bank employee completes the KYC/AML verification step. Status flows: Pending → Submitted → Approved / Rejected.

Customer journey
  1. Form appears automatically after accepting an offer
  2. Customer fills in any required fields not pre-populated
  3. Submit sends the form to the RM queue for review
  4. Customer receives a push notification when status changes
End-to-end flow summary

How it connects

The Admin Portal and Customer Portal share the same backend API. Actions in the admin panel (activating a product, approving an AI suggestion, changing a consent rule) take effect immediately for customers.

Built-in Compliance Guardrails

The engine enforces ten hard guardrails that cannot be overridden through configuration. These are implemented at the code level and logged in every recommendation record. Any recommendation that fails a guardrail is excluded and the reason code is stored in the audit trail.

# Guardrail What it enforces Regulation Failure action
G-01 Confidence threshold Model score must exceed 0.60 — low-confidence suggestions are never sent AI Act Art. 9 (risk management) Offer excluded, reason: low_confidence
G-02 Rate limiting Max 5 AI analysis calls per hour per endpoint to prevent cost runaway Internal policy HTTP 429, alert generated
G-03 Fragile customer protection Customers with financial_health = fragile cannot receive credit or lending products EBA Consumer Protection All lending excluded, reason: fragile_customer
G-04 Stale suggestion expiry AI-generated product suggestions auto-expire after 30 days if not actioned AI Act Art. 9 (monitoring) Status → expired, removed from queue
G-05 AI output content filter PII (email, phone, IBAN, card number) stripped from all AI-generated text GDPR Art. 25 (privacy by design) PII replaced with [REDACTED] tag, warning logged
G-06 Product catalogue cap Maximum 50 active products in the catalogue at any time Internal policy (model quality) New product activation blocked until one is deactivated
G-07 AI product cooldown AI-suggested products must complete a 24-hour staging period before going live AI Act Art. 14 (human oversight) Product held in staging state, not scored
G-08 Minor / restricted customer Customers flagged as minor or legally restricted cannot receive investment products MiFID II Art. 25 Investment products excluded, reason: customer_restricted
G-09 Risk profile mismatch High-risk products (ETF Growth, Managed Portfolio) require risk_tolerance ≥ moderate MiFID II Suitability Product excluded, reason: risk_mismatch
G-10 Prompt injection filter AI outputs scanned for injection patterns before being stored or displayed AI Act Art. 15 (cybersecurity) Pattern replaced with [FILTERED], warning logged

Architecture Summary

BankOffer AI is deployed on Kubernetes via a GitOps pipeline (ArgoCD). Each domain is owned by an autonomous Claude Code agent that coordinates through Git artifacts. The infrastructure is designed for zero-downtime deployment and horizontal scaling of every stateless component.
